Development of
Rukiga
District

We are grounded to the values of togetherness, fairness, humility, equity, justice, respect for individuals, inclusiveness and independence through freedom from religious and political affiliation.

We Operate in the countryside of Rukiga, South Western Uganda, where the illiteracy rate is 80% above the national average of 72.2%, teenage pregnancies at 34% and school dropouts are a bad daily dose with 20% of primary school age going children out of school which is above the national average of 12.5% , women and young people are excluded from decision making processes, many young people are still unemployed and living in a country where subsistence agriculture stands at 69.9% and human rights are treated as a luxury.

TARGETS

We target to halve the illiteracy rate in Rukiga from 80% to 65% by the end of 2025 and poverty from 23% that is above the national average of 19% to 18% by the year 2025, reduce youth and women unemployment rate from 83% to 50% by July 2025, reduce the teenage pregnancy rates from 34% that is over the national average of 25% to 23% by October 2025, reduce the number of primary school age going children who do not go to school from 20% that is above the national average of 12.5% to at least 12.5% by the end of 2025, increase the level of participation by young people and women in decision making and/or integration of their issues to at least 60% combined on Rukiga local Government committees by the end of 2025, increase the ratio of commercial farming to subsistence farming from 3:7 to 6:4 by the end of 2025 while ensuring food security.

OBJECTIVES:

Specifically, we look forward to:
  1. Promoting talents amongst young talented citizens in sports, drama and academics.
  2. Creating household income generating activities among members of the community.
  3. Reducing the rate of poverty in the community by creating awareness about government and non-government development programs.
  4. Supporting education programs for the youth and children in order to reduce illiteracy.
  5. Promoting agricultural schemes that help communities to sustain food security.
  6. Promoting human rights for a just, free and fair involvement of all community members.
